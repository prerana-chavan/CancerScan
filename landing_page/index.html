Detection Capability

Detecting the Two Most
Common Lung Cancers

ACA and SCC together represent 70% of all lung cancer cases worldwide. CancerScan covers what matters most clinically.

ACA
Adenocarcinoma
The most common lung cancer type, occurring in outer lung tissue. More common in non-smokers, women, and younger patients. Generally slower growing with better prognosis.
~40% of all lung cancers · 5yr survival: 63%
SCC
Squamous Cell Carcinoma
Found centrally in the lung near the bronchi. Strongly linked to heavy smoking and more aggressive — making early AI detection critically important.
~30% of all lung cancers · 5yr survival: 24%
✓
Benign / Non-Cancerous
CancerScan's gatekeeper model first validates the lung scan, then classifies as benign or malignant with high confidence — preventing unnecessary interventions for healthy patients.
Clear diagnosis · Prevents unnecessary treatment · High confidence scoring
